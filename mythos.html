One Minute

Block, after block, after block, after block breaks. The sounds of each are beginning to blend together. Just keep digging they said, it can’t be that long they said. Slimes were nowhere in sight, and chances were that an hour from now there would be none.

Ferris began to hear his own heartbeat in sync with the stone he was destroying. Just then, his pickaxe broke, but in a matter of seconds another had taken its place and the cycle continued. He broke block, after block, after block. Ferris could hear his breathing move in sync with the stone he was destroying. His pickaxe broke. Another took its place. He broke block, after block. Ferris could hear the sounds of his footsteps reverberate in sync with the stone he was destroying. His pickaxe broke. Another took its place. He broke a block, and the new pickaxe broke. It must have been damaged already.

Ferris reached all over, but there were no more pickaxes left. He began the walk back. For ten seconds, all that could be heard were his heartbeat, his breath, and his footsteps. Another ten seconds, were the sounds of louder, faster footsteps. Another ten seconds, were the screams of a horrified miner. Another ten seconds, were the screams of a horrified miner and something much louder. After another ten seconds, the sound of the miner falling to the ground sealed his fate. Before the final ten seconds of his journey could be completed, Ferris was gone, leaving a faint trail of red down the tunnel.

Faith

“Welcome child.”

The skeptic walked into the holy place, it seemed more like a residence than a church. “Who’re you?”

The speaker seemed offended, “Why, I am the speaker, I speak for the Jordan.”

“Yeah, and how does that work?” The skeptic analyzed the building’s interior. It was clear that it was built by no god, but a man, regardless of what the common citizens would believe.

“I have a connection with it, ever since I…”

“Since you built it?,” the skeptic interrupted.

“I- I have no idea what you are talking about. The Jordan was placed here by a higher power to guide us all.”

“Well, your followers may eat that up, but anyone with half a brain can tell it’s a ruse,” the skeptic leaned against a wall.

The speaker grew increasingly nervous as the skeptic spoke, “So you mean to try and defame me? Ruin the one thing that brings meaning to so many lives?”

“No, I’m here to tell you that a very powerful man has taken interest in your control over Section 8’s culture. He wants you to continue ‘educating’ the public, but now with his orders guiding you.”

“I have no interest in a partnership of any kind, so you can tell your boss to drop it.

The skeptic chuckled to himself, “This would be no partnership, he would become your new boss.”

“Is that supposed to convince me to change my mind?”

“No, I just like the details to be accurate,” he paused for a moment. “Listen, I strongly encourage you to take this deal.”

“Are you threatening me with something if I refuse?”

The skeptic stood up and pulled back his jacket, revealing a crossbow with a single firework loaded, “I’m afraid I’ve been instructed to.” The speaker was shocked at the skeptic’s actions, but he had worked too hard to get where he was to give it up now. “People like you and your boss think they can take everything by force, you think you’re so much tougher than all of us,” the speaker pulled a diamond sword from his waist, “but we have fight in us, and we won’t back down.” Now the speaker was smiling, he held out his sword waiting for the skeptic.

“It was your choice,” the skeptic reached for his crossbow. Many people in the area could confirm that they heard a loud boom that day. A short hour later, a cleaning crew followed by a construction crew entered the Jordan. Most assumed that it was getting renovated.

Perspective

Fear filled Archibald’s mind when the Mall began construction. Now demand for workers would be higher than ever. He was the only male in his unit. He had watched the handler take every last one of his children to work in the halls. Each and every time, there was nothing he could do, for the handler could overpower any one of them in an instant.

In June, news came in that a general store, Ikea, would be built in the mall. The handler visited Archibald’s unit and took with him all the children that had been born since he had last visited. Archibald’s rage grew, but he knew that there was little he could do. On his way out, the handler’s pants caught on an edge and caused him to drop a piece of iron. He did not seem to notice, but Archibald did. The item was quickly hidden under his bed.

In July, news came in that a bookstore, Bens & Noble, would be built in the mall. Once again, the handler visited Archibald’s unit and took all of the new born children with him. Archibald’s rage grew even more, but he knew there was little he could do. On his way out, the handler left a furnace because several people had died of hypothermia, hurting his quotas. The handler did not realize the opportunity he had just given them, but Archibald did. He made sure to thank the handler greatly for the gift.

In August, news came in that several workers at the mall had died, and their positions would need to be replaced. Once again, the handler visited Archibald’s unit and began to take the newborns. Archibald could stand by no longer, he rushed towards the handler to try and stop him; but just as he predicted, the handler overpowered him. That day, he was beaten almost to death by a wooden baton that the handler was carrying. When he was done, there was little of the baton left, so the handler tossed it into the nearby crops and left. The other people tended to Archibald’s wounds and tried to comfort him. But he was the most comfortable he had been in a long time, for his plan was ready.

In July, the handler returned to replace Archibald. After their altercation, he figured it would be safest to dispose of him. But upon entering his unit, Archibald was nowhere in sight. The handler was confused, he was met with a room full of women and children staring at him, seemingly in anticipation. Out of the corner of his eye, he spotted movement. As the handler began to turn to face his attacker, his head was cut clean from his body. Archibald led his unit outside and towards freedom.
